# Money System Overview
---
## The 4-account money system
The 4-account money system is a simple way to manage income by separating it into clear, purpose-driven categories. Instead of keeping all money in one place, each dollar is assigned a job as soon as it comes in.

The system consists of four core accounts:

Spending (Checking): Used for everyday expenses like bills, food, and personal purchases.
Taxes (High-Yield Savings): A dedicated account where a percentage of income (typically 25–30%) is set aside to cover future tax obligations.
Savings (Emergency Fund): Reserved for unexpected expenses such as car repairs, medical costs, or temporary loss of income.
Investing (Wealth Building): Long-term funds placed into accounts like a Roth IRA or brokerage account to grow over time.

By splitting income immediately upon receiving it, this system removes guesswork, prevents overspending, and ensures that taxes, emergencies, and future growth are consistently accounted for. It creates structure, discipline, and financial clarity without requiring complex budgeting.

## Using the System to Pay Taxes Efficiently

One of the biggest advantages of the 4-account system is how it simplifies paying taxes. By consistently setting aside a percentage of income (typically 25–30%) into a dedicated tax account, you avoid the stress of scrambling for money at the end of the year.

When this tax account is a high-yield savings account, it does more than just hold money—it earns interest over time. As your income is deposited and your tax portion is set aside, the balance gradually grows. The bank pays you interest for holding that money, effectively generating a small return on funds that you would have owed anyway.

By the end of the year, you have:

The full amount needed to pay your taxes
Additional money earned from interest

This means your tax obligation is fully covered, and the interest earned either offsets part of the tax burden or becomes extra money you keep. Instead of taxes feeling like a sudden loss, the system turns it into a controlled, predictable process that even works slightly in your favor.

The key is consistency—setting aside money immediately when income is received and leaving it untouched until taxes are due.
